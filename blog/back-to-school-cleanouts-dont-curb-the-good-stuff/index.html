Back-to-School Clean-Outs: Don't Curb the Good Stuff

July 24, 2026

Every August, the same thing happens across the OKC metro: back-to-school season kicks off the great bedroom purge. New school year, new start — and the kids' closets finally give up everything they've been hiding since 2019.

Most of what comes out really is donate-pile material. But every year, good stuff rides the purge straight to the curb because it surfaced on a Tuesday night when everyone was tired. Before the bags leave the house, here's the thirty-second filter.

Pull these aside before they hit the curb

  • Game consoles and handhelds — the Switch that got upgraded past, the dusty Wii, the DS in the junk drawer, and especially anything older that migrated from your own childhood: N64, GameCube, PlayStation 1/2, Game Boys.
  • The games themselves — bins of loose DS carts, stacks of scratched-ish discs, that one shoebox of Pokémon games (seriously, check for the Pokémon games).
  • Controllers, chargers, and accessories — even orphaned ones. The cables are worth more paired with the console they belong to, and we sort that out for you.
  • Action figures and toy lines — outgrown doesn't mean worthless. 2000s-era figures, playsets, and even fast-food promo toys have buyers.
  • The "whole bin" situation — mixed toys, games, and mystery cables in one tote? That's not a problem to solve. That's exactly what we buy.

The one-photo rule

You don't have to sort, test, or research any of it. That's the whole point. Take one photo of the pile — the open tote, the bed covered in stuff, whatever it looks like right now — and send it to the OK Retro Buys page on Facebook.

You'll get back a real cash offer for what we can use. Then we pick up or meet, OKC metro, one stop. The rest of the purge can go to the donate pile with a clear conscience — and the good stuff pays for the school supplies instead of vanishing into a landfill.

Clean-out season is short. The photo takes ten seconds. Send it before the curb gets there first. 🎒📸
