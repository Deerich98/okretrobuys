Pizza on the floor, sleeping bags nobody intends to sleep in, one TV, four controllers. If you grew up in the late 90s or 2000s, you already know the games — but which one was *THE* game says everything about your friend group. Ranked, scientifically, by chaos.

## 5. Mario Party (any of them)

The friendship-ender with dice. Nobody remembers who won; everybody remembers who stole whose star. Mario Party nights didn't end because the game ended — they ended because someone's mom said it was time to go home, mid-argument. Chaos rating: emotional.

## 4. Mario Kart 64 / Double Dash

The great equalizer. Your friend who was terrible at everything else somehow lived on Rainbow Road. Blue shells taught a generation about injustice. Double Dash added co-op karts, which meant you could now blame a *specific person* for the loss. Chaos rating: high, but affectionate.

## 3. GoldenEye 007

Four quadrants of screen and absolutely no honor. The house rules were always the same — **no Oddjob, no screen-peeking** — and they were always broken, in that order. Someone camped the body armor every single match and denied it every single time. Chaos rating: Geneva-Convention adjacent.

## 2. Super Smash Bros. Melee

The one that turned sleepovers into training camps. Somebody's older brother knew wavedashing and ruined everything. Items-on Final Destination purists and items-off tournament kids, same living room, irreconcilable worldviews. Melee nights are why some of us still flinch at the Home-Run Bat sound. Chaos rating: competitive scarring.

## 1. Halo (the LAN sleepover)

The endgame: two TVs, two Xboxes, a system-link cable somebody's dad definitely needed back, and eight kids yelling across a hallway. Blood Gulch at 2 a.m. is a core memory for an entire generation. Chaos rating: maximum — this one required *logistics*.
