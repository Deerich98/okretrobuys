Most people sitting on a box of old games or toys never sell it — not because they don't want the money, but because every route to the money looks like a hassle. Listing it yourself means photographing, researching, pricing, and shipping every piece. Local marketplace ads mean strangers, no-shows, and "is this still available?" purgatory.

So the box goes back in the closet. Here's the version without the hassle.

## The whole process, all three steps

**1. 📸 Send a photo.** Message the OK Retro Buys page on Facebook with a picture of what you've got. You don't need to know what anything is, sort it, or test it first. A photo of the open tote works. A shelf shot works. Blurry-ish works.

**2. 💬 Get a cash offer.** We identify what's in the photo and come back with a real number for the lot. No "bring it by and we'll see," no bait price that shrinks at pickup. If we need one more angle to price something fairly, we'll ask for one more photo — that's the extent of your homework.

**3. 🤝 We pick up or meet.** OKC metro. You hand over the stuff, you get paid, done in one stop. No packing peanuts anywhere in your life.

## What we're looking for

- **Consoles and handhelds** — retro to modern: NES through Switch, Game Boys, PSPs, the works
- **Games** — boxed or loose carts and discs, single titles or shelves of them
- **Controllers, accessories, and the cable spaghetti** that goes with them
- **Action figures and vintage toys** — 80s/90s/2000s lines, complete or not
- **Whole collections and mixed lots** — honestly our favorite; you don't have to sort a thing

Not sure something qualifies? Send the photo anyway. The answer costs you thirty seconds.

## Why we do it this way

Every step exists to remove the reason people *don't* sell: no listing labor, no stranger roulette, no shipping, no guessing games about value. You get a number while the box is still in your hand, and you decide with real information.

That tote you keep walking past is a message away from being cash. 📸 → 💬 → 🤝 — message OK Retro Buys on Facebook any time.
